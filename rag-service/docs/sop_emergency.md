# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) — Emergency Management

This document defines standard protocols for emergency scenarios at MetLife Stadium, including medical emergencies, structural fires, severe weather alerts, and security threats. All personnel must familiarize themselves with these guidelines and gate-specific evacuation routes.

## 1. Medical Emergencies
When a medical incident is reported or observed:
1. **Immediate Assessment**: First responder on scene must assess the severity of the emergency and determine if local first-aid is sufficient or if professional medical intervention is required.
2. **Notification**: Contact Stadium Control Room (SCR) immediately via channel 1 or call the hotline. Provide the exact location (section, row, seat, or gate) and a description of the symptoms (e.g., chest pain, unconsciousness, severe bleeding, fracture).
3. **First-Aid Dispatch**: SCR will immediately dispatch a mobile paramedic team. Maintain visual contact and keep the area clear for emergency services access.
4. **Gate-Specific Staging**:
   - **Gate A / West Side**: Medical Team Alpha (Staged at First-Aid Room Section 112).
   - **Gate B / North Side**: Medical Team Beta (Staged at First-Aid Room Section 128).
   - **Gate C / East Side**: Medical Team Gamma (Staged at First-Aid Room Section 143).
   - **Gate D / South Side**: Medical Team Delta (Staged at First-Aid Room Section 101).

## 2. Structural Fire Protocol
In the event of a fire or smoke report:
1. **Activate Alarm**: Pull the nearest manual fire alarm pull station.
2. **Alert SCR**: Inform SCR of the exact location and size of the fire.
3. **Isolate and Evacuate**: Direct fans immediately away from the affected area towards designated exit paths. Do not attempt to fight fires larger than a small wastebasket.
4. **Containment**: Close doors behind you during evacuation to contain smoke and heat.

## 3. Severe Weather and Lightning
If a severe weather warning (e.g., lightning strikes within an 8-mile radius, tornado warning, high-wind warning) is issued:
1. **Shelter in Place**: Direct fans from open seating bowls, plazas, and parking fields into covered concourse areas.
2. **Public Address Announcements**: SCR will initiate automated PA systems directing fans to seek shelter in the nearest concourse.
3. **Suspension of Play**: Match officials will suspend play. All stadium staff should guide fans away from metal structures, flagpoles, and open gate entrances.

## 4. Security Threats and Active Hostility
In the case of active threats or suspicious packages:
1. **Report**: Immediately alert Security Control with detailed descriptions (appearance of person/package, direction of travel, or exact location).
2. **Evacuation / Lockdown**: Under direction of the Security Commander, execute either a full stadium evacuation or local zone lockdown.
3. **Run, Hide, Fight**: Instruct staff and fans on the basic survival steps depending on proximity to the active threat.

## 5. Gate-Specific Evacuation Routes
In a full stadium evacuation, fans must be guided systematically to prevent bottlenecks and stampedes:
- **Gate A (West Exit)**: Evacuation route for Sections 100-120 and West concourse. Direct fans through Gate A out towards Parking Lot 3 and Lot 4. Wheelchair ramps are fully operational.
- **Gate B (North Exit)**: Evacuation route for Sections 121-135 and North concourse. Direct fans out towards the Shuttle Depot and Lot 5. Avoid structural underpasses.
- **Gate C (East Exit)**: Evacuation route for Sections 136-150 and East concourse. Direct fans to the East Plaza and onwards to public transit rail terminals.
- **Gate D (South Exit)**: Evacuation route for Sections 101-105, 151-160, and South concourse. Direct fans towards the southern recreational fields and Lot 1.
