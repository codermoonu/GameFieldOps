# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) — Crowd Control and Flow Management

This document defines standard protocols for managing fan traffic, gate queues, concourse bottlenecks, and crowd density during match events at MetLife Stadium.

## 1. Monitoring Gate Status and Wait Times
Stadium operators monitor entry gates in real-time. Each gate is categorized by its queue wait time and operational status:
1. **Flowing** (Wait time: 0–15 mins): Standard operations. Security lanes are moving smoothly.
2. **Congested** (Wait time: 16–30 mins): Elevating queue sizes. Direct staff to open secondary scanning lines.
3. **Bottleneck** (Wait time: >30 mins): Critical queues. Trigger automatic rerouting and open auxiliary gates.
4. **Incident** (Suspended): Gate is closed due to a localized emergency (e.g., medical incident, security event). Immediate rerouting required.

## 2. Threshold-Based Action Protocols
When a gate's queue wait time exceeds specific thresholds, operators must execute the following SOPs:
- **Wait Time > 15 minutes**:
  - Staff alert: Instruct supervisors at the gate to double-check scanner connectivity.
  - Add line-management staff to organize queue lines into neat zig-zag formations.
- **Wait Time > 20 minutes**:
  - Activate dynamic signage: Change digital billboards near approach paths to display wait times.
  - Deploy roaming volunteer teams to pre-check fan tickets and bags before they enter the formal queue.
- **Wait Time > 30 minutes (Bottleneck)**:
  - **Open Auxiliary Gates**: Open Gate D (South Auxiliary Gate) if not already active.
  - **Reroute Crowd Flow**: Deploy volunteer staff to guide arriving fans to alternate gates with lower wait times.
  - For Gate B bottleneck: Reroute fans to Gate A (West) or Gate D (South).
  - For Gate A bottleneck: Reroute fans to Gate B (North) or Gate D (South).
  - Broadcast push notifications to fans via the stadium app advising them of alternate gate entrances.

## 3. Incident-Response Rerouting Rules
If a gate is closed or set to "incident" status:
1. **Gate Closure**: Close all ticketing and security lanes at that gate immediately. Set up physical barricades.
2. **First Responder Access**: Clear a 20-meter corridor for emergency vehicle and personnel ingress/egress.
3. **Alternate Routing Guidance**:
   - **If Gate B is closed**: Direct all incoming fans to **Gate A** (West) or **Gate D** (South).
   - **If Gate A is closed**: Direct all incoming fans to **Gate B** (North) or **Gate D** (South).
   - **If Gate C is closed**: Direct all incoming fans to **Gate B** (North) or **Gate D** (South).
4. **Staff Roles**: Volunteers must stand at major walkways leading to the closed gate, holding signage and directing traffic away from the incident zone.

## 4. Density Management in Concourses
If crowd density in specific concourse zones reaches "High" or "Extreme" (>4 persons per square meter):
1. **One-Way Routing**: Implement temporary one-way walking loops to keep crowds moving.
2. **Concession Control**: Instruct concession stand security to manage queues so they do not spill into main transit pathways.
3. **Stairwell/Elevator Metering**: Station staff at the base of escalators and stairs to regulate the rate of fans ascending/descending.
